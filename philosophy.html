Teaching Philosophy

I believe that a teacher should adopt a learner-centered approach and act as a mentor to the students. He or she not only should be a good teacher but also should treat each student as a person to build their confidence and ensure their success in academic, professional, and personal lives.

I am driven by this philosophy whenever I communicate with my students in or outside of class. I always do my best to go the extra mile for my students to provide them innovative, effective, and personalized support to improve their performance.

From my experience as a student and an instructor, I know that students produce their best when they receive personalized guidance. This is particularly important in a skill-based class where students expect to receive adequate technological support.

Moreover, they give their best efforts when they can relate the coursework with their future career. Therefore, I provide them real-world examples while talking about the concepts. I make sure that the students understand the importance of the given tasks and set their learning goals accordingly. Apart from that, I work to create a comfortable environment in the classroom so that students do not hesitate to ask questions, engage with others, express opinions, or present new ideas.

My teaching method reflects my philosophy of personalized care. In small classrooms, I try to know each student individually and address their needs and concerns with empathy. I go beyond my teaching responsibilities to genuinely help my students, which often include conducting one-on-one sessions outside of my office hours and providing additional support to underachieving students.

Student evaluations reflect success of my teaching style. One student commented in his/her course evaluation: “Dr. Isa offered real world advice for dealing with situations that could be potentially difficult for video journalists. He was very professional and understanding and receptive to everyone's questions and ideas.” Another student wrote: “I really enjoyed this class and am forever grateful for it! The professor was very helpful and I would definitely take another course with him.”
